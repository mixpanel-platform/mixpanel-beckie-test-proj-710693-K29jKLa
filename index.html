Getting Started with Mixpanel

A comprehensive guide for kicking off your Mixpanel instance for new customers.

Roles and Responsibilities

In order to have a successful Mixpanel launch, you'll need to understand the different responsibilities and tasks needed before you have a tailored analytics instance for your business. Below are the tasks and roles that commonly assume each piece of the project.

  • Product Manager: formalize scope of project (via spec), determine KPIs, provisioning access, learn Mixpanel core-reports
  • Marketing: consult on spec (people!), articulate marketing needs, author notifications, learn (basic) Mixpanel reports, connect acquisition data (if available)
  • Developer: implementing mixpanel (dev/prod), testing implementation (live view), accountable to implementation spec
  • Analyst (if applicable): learn core-mixpanel reports and capabilities
  • Executive (if applicable): approve implementation spec, articulate business/technology needs (beyond core analytics strategy), consult on spec

Understanding Events and People

Mixpanel has two fundamental spheres:

  • Events: the things people do in your app
  • People: the users who use your application

Both 'spheres' have properties which describe them (a 'sign up' can come from facebook; a 'user' can be 22 years old)
Everything that gets sent to Mixpanel is either an event, or a person, or a property underneath. Mixpanel can accept data from anywhere, but it must fit into one of these two paradigms. Therefore every product that uses Mixpanel to track its data needs to be adapted to fit into this model.
Mixpanel is the leading analytics platform because we allow users to close the loop on their analytics strategies. That is, you actively test your hypotheses and act on them directly within the platform. The two ways you can practice closed-loop analytics are:
  • Notifications: to PEOPLE based on their properties
  • A/B tests: to PEOPLE, also based on their properties
Closed loop analytics can only be practice based on the events, properties, and people attributes you have sent to Mixpanel.
Implementation Spec
An Implementation Spec is where you keep track of the events, people, and properties that you are sending to Mixpanel. It's essentially a project plan -- a document you can hand off to your developers to implement, as well as a reference guide for what you've implemented and what you'd like to add or remove in the future.
